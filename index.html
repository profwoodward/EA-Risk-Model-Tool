Companion to: Quantifying Compromise Risk in Exceptional Access Architectures Under Deep Uncertainty

The Risk-Side
Question Set

A structured elicitation tool. Twelve questions over architectural scope, adversary environment, operational controls, deployment, and decision framework — each mapped to a parameter in the underlying model.

The output is a range under the assumptions you specify. Structural findings (architectural ordering, tail-shape direction) do not change with your answers; calibration-conditional magnitudes do. Read the outputs accordingly.

Specification

§A · Architectural scope
Q1Which architectural class is under consideration?

Selects the structural model and its prior calibrations.

Q2How many operators host the EA infrastructure?

Parameterises the concentration modifier δ_concen.

Q3Does the mandate add new infrastructure or modify pre-existing?

Clarifies the no-EA counterfactual interpretation; affects output annotation.

§B · Adversary environment
Q4Which adversary tiers are operationally relevant?

Parameterises the APT mixture weight w^APT.

Q5Is the operator a known APT-priority target?

Modulates targeting premium within the [3×, 15×] range.

§C · Operational controls
Q6Are HSMs mandated with prescriptive audit?

Parameterises δ_EA and the zero-day channel rate.

Q7Are operational procedures prescribed or discretionary?

Parameterises the operational-error channel rate P_ops.

Q8Is key custody compartmentalised or unified?

Parameterises δ_concen adjustment and γ_X/γ.

§D · Forward secrecy
Q9Does the architecture preserve forward secrecy?

Consequence-side flag; affects output interpretation, not the probability model.

§E · Deployment horizon & decision framework
Q10What is the deployment horizon?

Parameterises T in cumulative projections.

Q11Is the mandate subject to periodic reassessment?

Modulates stationarity caveat strength.

Q12Under which decision framework are outputs weighted?

Selects which output is foregrounded in the risk profile card.

Robustness signals
Structural: EA-equipped architecture carries higher risk than the no-EA counterfactual within this class. This finding is invariant under your answers, conditional on δ ≥ 1.
Cross-architectural: T-EA exceeds OTT-EA at the median; OTT-EA can exceed T-EA in the upper tail under correlated campaigns.
Calibration-conditional: The specific magnitudes above reflect the parameter ranges implied by your answers. The ranges shown integrate over the defensible prior calibration sweep.

Sensitivity

How the FH upper bound and Bayesian 95th percentile of the annual compromise rate respond when individual answers are toggled, holding all others fixed at your current specification.

Quantitative outputs are calibrated to the framework's headline values; this tool is a structured interpolation of the underlying model, not a re-derivation. Structural findings (architectural ordering, tail-shape direction) are invariant under the answer set; calibration-conditional magnitudes scale with the modifier aggregate.
